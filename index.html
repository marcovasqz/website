  • About myself

    I am a go-getter, originally from Venezuela where I graduated as a Production Engineer but have always been passionate about computers since I was a child. With the pandemic, I decided to turn this passion into my vocation and It’s the best decision I’ve made in my life. I always knew I wanted to work in cyber security but I needed base knowledge/experience before I could put a foot in the door. So I got an IT technician job where I did absolutely everything, from help desk support to incident response since power outages are not uncommon in Black Hawk. I got promoted to tier 2 just before a recruiter reached out to me about the job I’m currently in.

    I couldn’t say no since my next step in my cyber security path was to get experience with networking. My current role as a Network DevTest Engineer is all I needed. I use my knowledge to find unwanted behaviors, isolate issues by reading and analyzing logs. I utilize my understanding of network protocols and how computers work to simulate all kinds of situations and provide detailed information about bugs in freshly developed firmware for Spectrum home routers, which use a linux-based operating system and don’t have any GUI.

    I have always received compliments from employers about my work ethic and how quick I learn. I am passionate about computers so my learning experience is really enjoyable. I am detail driven with an eye for spotting errors. My analytical skills are sharp and I am able to solve complex problems. Despite being the new employee, I am often the go-to person on staff who gets assigned complicated cases since I do love challenges.
